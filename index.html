The Basics

Ultimate frisbee is a bit like a hybrid between soccer and football. It's played with seven people per team on a grass, turf or sand field. Players throw the disc to one another and score by catching it in the endzone.

  1. The Idea

    1968

    Joel Silver introduces the idea of Ultimate Frisbee at Columbia High School in New Jersey.

  2. The First Game

    1969

    High schoolers play the first organized game of ultimate. They use the school parking lot for their field and piles of coats to mark the endzones.

  3. Level Up

    November 6, 1972

    Rutgers beats Princeton 29-27 in the first college ultimate game.

  4. Tournament Central

    April 25, 1975

    Eight teams take part in the first ultimate tournament, called The National Collegiate Championships.

  5. Going Global

    1983

    The first World Ultimate Championship tournament is held in Sweden. USA sends club teams to compete against European national teams and wins both the open and womens divisions.

  6. Exhibitionism

    1989

    Ultimate is showcased as an exhibition sport during the World Games.

  7. World Games

    2001

    Mixed ultimate is officially added to the World Games medal sports. Canada wins the gold medal over the USA.

  8. Olympic Recognition

    August 2, 2015

    The International Olympic Committee grants full recognition to the World Flying Disc Federation, the international governing body for the sport of ultimate. This raises hopes amongst the community that the sport will someday be included in the Olympic Games.

The Divisions

There are four primary divisions in ultimate in the US: youth, college, club and professional. At most levels, there are three gender divisions: women's, mixed and men's.

The Icons

Although success is transient for many ultimate teams, there are some teams that dominate year after year.

< BackNext >

Fury was founded in 1997 in the San Francisco Bay Area. The team won a national championship for seven straight years, from 2006 to 2012. For more, see Fury's website.

Riot is based in Seattle, arguably the strongest hub of ultimate in the world. Since 2001, Riot has never placed lower than 3rd place at club nationals. For more about Riot, see their website.

Molly Brown, in Colorado, is full of big name talent. MB has drawn power players from club teams across the country, and most recently recruited Colombian all-star Manuela Cardenas. For more, see the team's Facebook page.
